Matt Andres

PhD Student in Ecology, Evolution, and Behavior

University of Tennessee, Knoxville

Research Interests

I am a PhD student interested in studying sensory ecology, social behaviors, and disease ecology. My research focuses on how animals use olfaction and other chemical senses to gather information from their environment, and how this sensory information drives behavior.

My primary interest lies in studying these phenomena in bats, particularly vampire bats and other members of the family Phyllostomidae. I am especially interested in how olfaction might serve as a mechanism for disease detection in vampire bats. To explore this, I plan to characterize odor profiles using chemical analysis and conduct behavioral experiments to investigate how vampire bats respond to different odors.

About My Work

In my research, I explore the ways in which animals navigate their complex environments using chemical cues. I am particularly interested in the role of olfaction in social behavior and disease ecology. By understanding how vampire bats use olfactory cues to potentially detect diseases, my work aims to shed light on broader ecological and evolutionary processes, with important implications for conservation and disease management in bat populations.
